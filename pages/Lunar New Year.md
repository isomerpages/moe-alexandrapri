<h4><strong>Lunar New Year 2026</strong></h4>
<p>During the 2026 Lunar New Year celebration, students at ALPS experienced
the rich traditions of the festival through engaging MTL CCE lessons and
a series of school-wide activities. Students participated in a variety
of meaningful experiences, including a parent-child lantern-making activity,
a traditional food showcase organised by our Parent Support Group (PSG),
writing Lunar New Year greetings, and festive craft-making activities conducted
in classrooms. Our Primary 6 students also took part in a meaningful Lo
Hei session, which provided opportunities to learn about the cultural significance
and values behind this tradition.</p>
<p></p>
<p>To create a festive atmosphere, members of the PSG and parent volunteers
thoughtfully decorated the school with horse-themed displays, transforming
the campus into a vibrant celebration space. Through these activities,
students deepened their appreciation and understanding of Chinese traditions
and customs while experiencing the joy of shared cultural celebrations.
The event also strengthened our school-home partnership, reflecting ALPS’
commitment to nurturing students through collaborative community efforts.</p>
<p></p>
<p>As ALPS welcomes the Year of the Horse with hope and positivity, we look
forward to continuing to provide authentic learning experiences that enrich
students’ cultural understanding and values.</p>
