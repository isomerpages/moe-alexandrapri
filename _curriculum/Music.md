<h4><strong>Programmes / Activities for Music Programme @ ALPS</strong></h4>
<p></p>
<p><strong>Recess Busking</strong>
</p>
<p>Held once a term, recess busking is an informal avenue where students
can showcase their musical talent to their peers. Through such platforms,
our student performers will be able to harness their instrumental skills
and build confidence through performance. Student audience will also pick
up performance etiquette tips and music appreciation during busking.</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong>Recess Play @ the MusicKingdom</strong>
</p>
<p>Students can play with the outdoor instruments at the MusicKingdom during
recess play. They can hone their music making skills through unstructured,
collaborative play, or just explore the timbres of the instruments available
there.</p>

<p><strong>Music Activities during International Friendship Week.</strong>
</p>
<p>The aim of the International Friendship Week programme is to allow students
to learn about the cultures and practices of people from different countries.
They can learn more about the musical traditions, as well as have a hands-on
session on the instruments.</p>

<p><strong>Key Music Programmes:</strong>
</p>
<p><strong>Lower Primary:</strong> The students are taught the fundamental
elements of music in Primary 1 and 2. The focus would be on percussive
instruments to provide the beat and rhythm of music, as well as singing.
They will create a soundscape based on stimulus given to them. The seeds
of cooperation and creativity are planted through group compositions and
performances in class.</p>
<p></p>
<p><strong>Middle Primary:</strong> The Primary 3 and 4 students learn how
to play the recorder, ukulele, and continue to develop their percussion
and singing skills as an ensemble. They will begin their first foray into
digital song writing and chiptunes production using Chrome Music Lab.</p>
<p></p>
<div class="isomer-image-wrapper">

<p><strong>Upper Primary:</strong> The students will compose their own song
and lyrics in a songwriting module. They are taught to use the GarageBand
app on the iPad to create music in a jam band. The students learn the skills
of critical and inventive thinking, as well as collaborative learning through
these modules.</p>
